# Notice

## Research status

CPCS-MX is a proposed research and engineering framework. It combines established concepts, active research, current platform documentation, and original operational conventions. Evidence labels in the paper and RAG corpus identify these categories.

The package does not provide medical, clinical, ergonomic, legal, safety-engineering, or real-world combat advice. Biomechanical examples are illustrative unless a cited source and calibration context establish otherwise.

The package includes titles, authors, publication metadata, short summaries, stable source IDs, and links for third-party materials. It does **not** include copies of copyrighted books, standards, proprietary manuals, subscription publications, vendor SDKs, or source videos.

## Examples and numerical values

All included performances and characters are fictional. Dense arrays are synthetic parser and hash-validation assets. Thresholds, weights, tolerances, effort proxies, and style multipliers are project conventions unless explicitly identified as externally established measurements.

Virtual-superhuman fields are designed for fictional animation. Anatomical limits, rig-safe limits, virtual limits, deformation, camera treatment, and VFX are deliberately separated. A stylized extension value must not be interpreted as safe human joint motion.

## Rights, identity, and privacy

Users are responsible for obtaining rights and consent for video analysis, motion capture, likeness, voice, character, choreography, brand, and dataset use. High-fidelity structural transfer should distinguish retained timing or grammar from identities, protected character designs, dialogue, music, logos, and source-specific expression.

## No warranty

The materials are supplied for research and prototyping without a guarantee that a target animation or video-generation system can execute every control. Unsupported fields must be surfaced by adapters rather than silently treated as enforced.
